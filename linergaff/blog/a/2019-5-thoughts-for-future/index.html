DIY 4: Thoughts for the Future

diy ~ 2019-03-15

This is specifically about the future of the product. 1. What it needs 2. How to get it there

Known Bugs

  • Bunching in back can cause pinching

this coudl be solved by a different cut of the fabric. Somethign that narrows as it goes back might not bunch the same way. A more experienced garment designer might easily come up with a solution.

  • It is not perfect in water

Fabric changes might help deal with swimming. There is something called Fabrifoam which might do okay. Also some kind of rubber might help here too.

  • The waist band sometimes slips down

This could be solved by making the elastic a T shape. Ideall this is resolved with a temporary/removable feature. However triyng to get it together like that has been difficult. Another option is to make the waist band out of two elastics. One is the small loop which goes in the fabric and up the butt. The other loops through the elastic and goes around the front. This does two things, it makes the removeable waist band cleaner (as the other elastic will sit in the butt) and thus wearable for multiple days. It also means there is a permanent elastic attached to the panel.

Improvement ideas

Wasit band needs updating. The elastic I use now is merely the best I have found so far. Ideally it is thinner but just as strong, and maybe even more comfortable. However if you use a different elastic for the butt cheeks, the comfort of the elastic is less important.

Better construction method for placement of eyes on panel. Currently my methods are limited to my knoweldge. The placement of the eye does seem to effect the visual of it as well as the function. Placing the eye right on the edge does seem to lessen the effect of the comfort band some. But placing it farther in makes it a bit more bulky to look at. Therefore, updating the shape and style of the comfort band alpongside this may make a big difference.

I have long thought to put an elastic down the middle of the panel. This extra elastic may smooth out the look. IF the fabric is too elastic, the shaft of the penis can often cause it to buldge in that place, however an elastic there might help smooth it out. I have never gotten this to work well.

Side seams may help the durability. By running a seam down the side of the panel it may last longer. But still leave a channel open for the elastic. IF you update to use 2 elastics, this is acutally easier. But if still only one, you have to thread a smaller hole every time in the future.

2 different fabrics may also provide some benefits. One fabric for the oputside one for the inside. The inside one could be used for comfort and the outside for proper tensioning. Or the inside could be specialized for athletics, water, or something else. Or the outside one could be used for aesthetics and the inside for structure. The biggest limiting factor here is stretch. The least stretchy fabric will win out in terms of tension, which may be hard with lace. You could also choose a different fabric for the inside of the comfort band but nowhere else. This could be used to try and create more stability in the tuck. AS, this is the palce where it is most liekly to slip first.
